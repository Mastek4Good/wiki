# Workshop Session: Actions, Outcomes and Reporting

**Date:** Wednesday 24 June 2026

Breakout 3 focused on how Simon on the Streets staff record actions and outcomes after outreach interactions.

The group discussed how to reduce the amount of admin staff need to complete later in the office, while still keeping human review and control before information becomes part of the formal record.

## What the Group Discussed

Staff often complete much of their action and outcome recording after outreach interactions, once they are back in the office.

This takes significant time and can mean staff have to reconstruct details from memory or informal notes.

The group explored whether quick text or audio capture could reduce this burden, especially if key details could later be:

* Extracted from the note.
* Associated with the right person.
* Pre-filled into an action or outcome form.
* Reviewed before submission.

## Evidence from the Discussion

The group described the current problem as staff needing to record information and outcomes after street-based conversations, but often having to return to the office and spend "quite a lot of hours" doing admin.

The aim was framed as reducing admin time so staff can spend more time helping people rather than staying in the office.

## Recording and Consent

An initial idea was to use a recording device and AI transcription, but the group identified two major issues:

* Staff would need consent from the person being recorded.
* Recording someone directly could feel intrusive or triggering, especially if the person has past experiences with police or authority figures.

The group then considered a less intrusive version. Staff could step aside after the conversation and send a quick text or voice note using WhatsApp or a similar messaging tool.

This would preserve the benefit of quick capture without putting a device in front of the person being supported.

## Building on Existing Behaviour

The group noted that Simon on the Streets already uses WhatsApp informally to share information.

There may be value in building on an existing behaviour rather than introducing something entirely unfamiliar.

However, the group identified several constraints:

* The WhatsApp API is paid.
* Costs should be kept low where possible.
* Any workaround would need to be legal, safe, and secure.
* More information is needed before choosing a specific platform.

## Possible Capture Flow

The group mapped a possible flow for turning quick field notes into structured records:

```text
Text / audio / details
-> extract data or identify keywords
-> associate with the right person
-> pre-populate form
-> review
-> submit
```

Another post-it proposed a speech-to-text option, either:

* Within a new or existing app.
* Integrated into WhatsApp.

## Summary Insight

The strongest idea was to create a safer "capture now, review before submit" workflow.

This would allow staff to quickly record a text or audio note after an interaction, then use extraction or keyword identification to help pre-fill the formal action or outcome record.

The worker would still review, edit, and submit the information before it became part of the official record.

This could reduce end-of-day admin and lower the risk of forgetting details, while keeping human control over accuracy, consent, and sensitive information.

## Possible MVP Direction

A lightweight post-interaction capture tool where a worker can add a short text or audio note after an outreach conversation.

The system could then create a draft action or outcome record for the worker to review, edit, and confirm before submission.

## Open Questions

Questions to explore next include:

* Is WhatsApp appropriate, or should this be a separate secure messaging-style tool?
* What information should be extracted from a voice note or text?
* Who reviews and confirms the extracted information?
* What should happen if consent has not been given?
* What information should never be recorded this way?
* How much would WhatsApp API integration cost?
* Are there safer or cheaper alternatives?
* How would this connect to formal reporting fields?
